Featured Impact Story

Food-Grain Quality Inspection for APMCs

Agricultural inspections have historically relied heavily on manual, subjective grading criteria. Outcomes fluctuated dynamically based on individual human judgment, which diminished trading standardisation and led to non-transparent, unpredictable pricing thresholds for farmers within localized marketplaces.

To bridge this baseline technology gap, the NASSCOM AI CoE successfully facilitated the scalable digital adoption of an advanced computer vision platform engineered by Upjao. The system processes raw images using machine learning networks to comprehensively evaluate more than ten discrete physical parameters—such as identifying broken kernels or extracting foreign organic material fractions—generating certifiable output datasets in under 30 seconds.

On-Ground Implementations & Outcomes:

  • Grassroots Deployments: Seamlessly integrated and scaled across APMC hubs situated in Anand and Kheda, Gujarat.
  • Ecosystem Traction: Directly empowered over 1,000 regional farming operators.
  • Value Realized: Facilitated verified algorithmic grain commerce transactions exceeding ₹700+ crores while decreasing revenue leaks from random batch rejections.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. How departments engage with AI CoE? What is the approach?

Government departments engage with AI CoE through a structured approach:

Understanding the Challenge and Identifying AI Opportunities
  • Understand in detail about the existing departmental processes
  • Identify inefficiencies and functions where AI can add value
  • Discuss with key stakeholders, define clear AI objectives, expected outcomes, and priorities
Finding the Right AI Solutions
  • Finalise functional requirements and success KPIs
  • Identify and evaluate suitable AI startups and solution providers
  • Support the department in selecting the right startup to develop the solution
Implementing AI Solutions
  • Support proof-of-concept and pilot deployments
  • Ensure alignment with IT and operational teams
2. If, as a decision maker of a government department, I go ahead with AI adoption, what kind of ROI should I expect and by when?

Adopting AI across the department can improve speed, precision, and overall process quality. The return on investment varies by use case and depends on your requirements.
