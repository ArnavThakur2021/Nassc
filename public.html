Empowering Public Services with AI

The Centre helps departments identify, validate, and implement AI solutions that make governance and public services easier and more efficient . It acts as an ecosystem enabler — understanding departmental processes , strategising AI adoption , and guiding end-to-end deployment.

AI CoE also conducts workshops and training sessions for government officials to help them understand and drive AI projects — with customised training on AI applications in governance covering both administrative and technical roles.

Understanding the Challenge

Understand existing departmental processes, identify inefficiencies where AI can add value, and define clear objectives with key stakeholders.

Finding the Right Solution

Finalise functional requirements and success KPIs. Identify, evaluate, and select the right AI startup or solution provider for the department.

Implementing AI Solutions

Support proof-of-concept and pilot deployments, ensuring full alignment with IT and operational teams for smooth rollout.

Impact Stories

Food-Grain Quality Inspection for APMCs

Manual grain inspection causes inconsistent, unfair pricing for farmers, requiring an AI computer-vision system evaluating ten-plus quality parameters within seconds.

  • AI CoE facilitated the AI-based grain quality inspection deployed at APMCs in Kheda and Anand, enabling 30-second grading, empowering 1,000+ farmers, and facilitating ₹700+ crore in fair trade.
  • The solution has been further deployed at 10 locations across the state by Gujarat State Civil Supplies Corporation Limited during paddy procurement for the PDS scheme benefitting around 3,300 farmers.
  • 5-7% avg. price improvements for participating farmers

Intelligent Document Processing for Scalable Automation


Built a production-ready bilingual (English–Gujarati) document processing system for accurate OCR, classification, and data extraction across diverse document types.

  • Processed 3,000+ documents saving 750 hours — 12–17 minutes saved per page, 150× faster data extraction with 100% system uptime.
  • Demonstrated extraction of 17+ fields across document types.
  • Delivered 96% classification accuracy with results in under 3 seconds across four categories.
  • Tested four OCR pipelines for English and Gujarati (including handwritten Gujarati) and provided recommendations.

Face Recognition, Attendance & Real-Time Person Tracking


Enabled centralized surveillance, automated attendance, and intelligent analytics for improved security, monitoring, and operational efficiency.

  • Achieved 98.2% overall face recognition accuracy with 0% false positives, ensuring highly reliable identification and monitoring.
  • Enabled automated attendance and real-time tracking, eliminating manual processes and improving workforce visibility.
  • Strengthened security through real-time alerts and classification (authorized, unauthorized, blacklisted, unknown) across all monitored zones.

Use Cases Pursued In Public Services

Sr. Use-Case Title Nominated By Description
1 AI Based Emergency Call Analytics Home Dept. The use case focuses on leveraging AI to identify fake calls made to Emergency Response Support System (ERSS) 112. By analyzing call recordings and extracting Call Data Records (CDR), AI-powered speech recognition detects specific keywords, patterns, or anomalies in spoken words that may indicate a hoax. The system flags such calls in real-time and alerts authorities to prioritize genuine emergencies, improving response efficiency and reducing operational strain on emergency services.
2 Object Identification and Tagging Urban Development Department Government departments struggle to manually monitor cattle across cities and districts, leading to inefficiencies and inaccuracies. This AI-based solution automates cattle identification and tagging in images and videos using deep learning techniques like CNNs and transformers, supporting real-time processing and large datasets. Applications include surveillance, infrastructure monitoring, and public safety. Deliverables include an AI model, documentation, and a demo. The solution improves decision-making, boosts efficiency, and establishes a scalable, accurate framework for intelligent cattle monitoring across urban and rural regions.
3 Trend, Seasonality, and Anomaly Detection Statistics Department Government departments rely on structured and time-series data for decision-making, yet identifying trends, seasonality, and anomalies manually is inefficient and error-prone. This AI-powered solution automates data analysis to uncover hidden patterns, detect anomalies, and enhance forecasting accuracy. Using advanced AI/ML techniques such as LSTM, ARIMA, and ensemble learning, the model ensures real-time insights via a dashboard. The solution supports large datasets, missing values, and diverse data types while adhering to security regulations. By improving operational efficiency and resource allocation, this innovation drives data-driven policymaking, ensuring timely trend detection for better governance and strategic planning.
4 Automated Blueprint Evaluation Town Planning & Valuation Department Develop an AI-based solution for CAD drawings that assists Town Planning and Validation Department (TPVD) in drafting, analyzing, and speedy finalization of Town Planning Schemes (TPS). The system should automate the evaluation of TPS to reduce manual review time, increase accuracy in decision-making, and enhance the efficiency of planning processes.
5 AI-Driven Wetland Intelligence & Digital Platform for Nal Sarovar Bird Sanctuary (Ramsar Site) Forest & Environment Department The project aims to build an AI-powered digital intelligence platform using multi-year ecological data collected from Nalsarovar Bird Sanctuary — a designated Ramsar site in Gujarat. Leveraging historical physico-chemical and biological data published by GEER Foundation, the solution will digitize, analyze, and visualize wetland parameters, provide trend analysis, and predict critical ecological indicators. The platform will serve as a scientific tool for informed decision-making, policy design, and resource optimization in wetland management.
6 AI Powered Theft Detection in Electricity Distribution Network GUVNL Electricity theft remains a persistent challenge in the power sector, leading to substantial commercial losses, unstable supply, and increased operational costs. Traditional theft detection relies heavily on manual audits, delayed billing reconciliations, and consumer complaints. These methods are reactive, slow, and often ineffective in identifying theft in real-time. The absence of a smart, automated, and scalable theft detection system prevents utility providers from proactively addressing theft hotspots, leading to unbilled consumption and significant revenue leakage.
7 AI-Powered Smart CCTV Surveillance System Food and Civil Supplies The Department seeks an AI-powered Smart CCTV Surveillance System to automate real-time monitoring across 250+ foodgrain godowns. Startups are expected to build an intelligent solution that detects unauthorized vehicles/persons, animal intrusion, unallocated foodgrain movement, delays in dispatch, and camera blackout/tampering. The system must generate instant alerts, enhance transparency, and reduce pilferage while improving operational efficiency across the Public Distribution System (PDS).
8 AI-Enabled Industrial Effluent & Emission Monitoring Gujarat Pollution Control Board The Board seeks an AI-driven system to automate compliance monitoring using real-time OEMS and CEMS data. Startups must build a model that detects anomalies, predicts potential violations, and flags non-compliance without relying on manual inspections or drone/satellite inputs. The solution should integrate multi-source industrial datasets, generate compliance scores and dashboards, and support scalable, proactive monitoring to strengthen pollution control and enforcement efficiency.
9 AI-Powered AR/VR Heritage Engagement using 3D Mapping Directorate of Archeology The Department is seeking an AR/VR-based solution to digitally recreate heritage sites—starting with Kera Temple—for immersive exploration. The system should use existing 3D scans to build accurate visualisations and allow visitors to experience the site virtually, understand its history, and interact with key architectural elements. The goal is to support preservation, enhance public engagement, improve accessibility for remote visitors, and create a scalable digital heritage model for future sites.
10 AI Based Monitoring of Anganwadi Infrastructure Projects Women and Child Development A large statewide network of over 50,000 Anganwadi centres is undergoing continuous construction, renovation, and maintenance, but current monitoring relies on manual field reports and periodic photo uploads, leading to inaccurate status updates, delayed interventions, and weak quality control. Reported progress often does not match on-ground reality, making planning and fund utilization reactive rather than data-driven. The required solution is an AI-powered infrastructure monitoring system that uses drone or satellite imagery and IoT data to automatically verify project progress, detect deviations, and generate real-time alerts and dashboards. The goal is to enable transparent, scalable, and predictive project tracking for faster decisions and better resource utilization.
11 Analytics of Poshan Tracker Data for Identification of Growth Risks Women and Child Development Large-scale maternal and child health data is collected across over 50,000 field centres through a nutrition tracking platform, but insights remain limited due to manual analysis and static dashboards. As a result, critical trends like undernutrition, anemia, stunting, and growth risks are often identified late, delaying targeted interventions. The required solution is an AI-driven analytics layer that can process high-volume health and nutrition data in real time, detect anomalies, predict risk patterns, and identify vulnerable populations early. Using machine learning and predictive modeling, the system should generate actionable insights, trend forecasts, and region-wise risk indicators to enable faster, evidence-based decisions and more effective nutrition program outcomes.
12 Automated Order Drafting System Special Secretary Revenue Department A high volume of land revenue appeal orders is currently drafted manually, leading to inconsistent language, formatting variations, interpretation gaps, and avoidable legal escalations. The absence of standardized templates and automated drafting tools results in repetitive effort, longer turnaround times, and non-uniform government orders. The required solution is an AI-assisted order drafting system that uses structured templates, dynamic inputs, OCR, and language models to automatically generate legally consistent, well-formatted orders in Gujarati. The system should provide smart suggestions, enforce departmental style and terminology, and integrate with existing case and land record systems—enabling faster, accurate, and uniform order generation at scale.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. How do departments engage with AI CoE? What is the approach?

Government departments engage with AI CoE through a structured approach:

Understanding the Challenge and Identifying AI Opportunities
  • Understand in detail the existing departmental processes
  • Identify inefficiencies and functions where AI can add value
  • Discuss with key stakeholders, define clear AI objectives, expected outcomes, and priorities
Finding the Right AI Solutions
  • Finalise functional requirements and success KPIs
  • Identify and evaluate suitable AI startups and solution providers
  • Support the department in selecting the right startup to develop the solution
Implementing AI Solutions
  • Support proof-of-concept and pilot deployments
  • Ensure alignment with IT and operational teams
2. As a decision maker, what kind of ROI should I expect from AI adoption and by when?

Adopting AI across the department can improve speed, precision, and overall process quality. The return on investment varies by use case and depends on your specific requirements.
