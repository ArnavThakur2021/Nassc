Impact Stories

Food-Grain Quality Inspection for APMCs

This use case focused on grain quality inspection, where evaluation was largely manual and subjective. Because assessments depended on individual judgment, outcomes were often inconsistent. This lack of standardisation reduced transparency in quality grading and contributed to unfair pricing for farmers, especially at local marketplaces.

At NASSCOM AI CoE, we facilitated the digital adoption of an AI-powered grain quality assessment solution developed by Upjao. The solution uses computer vision to assess more than ten physical parameters of grains — such as broken grains and foreign material — and delivers results within 30 seconds. The key shift was moving from subjective inspection to a scientific, data-driven process.

The system was deployed at APMCs in Kheda and Anand in Gujarat, enabling more objective quality evaluation at the grassroots level and improving transparency in pricing decisions.

  • 1,000+ farmers empowered through objective quality grading
  • Grain trade worth ₹700+ crore facilitated
  • Losses from quality-related rejections significantly reduced

Frequently Asked Questions

1. How do departments engage with AI CoE? What is the approach?

Government departments engage with AI CoE through a structured approach:

Understanding the Challenge and Identifying AI Opportunities
  • Understand in detail the existing departmental processes
  • Identify inefficiencies and functions where AI can add value
  • Discuss with key stakeholders, define clear AI objectives, expected outcomes, and priorities
Finding the Right AI Solutions
  • Finalise functional requirements and success KPIs
  • Identify and evaluate suitable AI startups and solution providers
  • Support the department in selecting the right startup to develop the solution
Implementing AI Solutions
  • Support proof-of-concept and pilot deployments
  • Ensure alignment with IT and operational teams
2. As a decision maker, what kind of ROI should I expect from AI adoption and by when?

Adopting AI across the department can improve speed, precision, and overall process quality. The return on investment varies by use case and depends on your specific requirements.
