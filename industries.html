Featured Industry Impact Story

Automating Text Ingestion for Complex Business Invoices

Manual processing of incoming multi-format corporate logistics documents has historically introduced data ingestion delays, human transcription errors, and resource allocation bottlenecks across accounting and back-office functions.

To address this core efficiency gap, the NASSCOM AI CoE supported the successful evaluation and deployment of an advanced document intelligence platform engineered by CogniFirst. The system employs customized natural language processing algorithms alongside layout-aware OCR text parsing layers to read unstructured invoice components, extract line items automatically, and validate cross-system balances within a centralized database framework.

Measurable Industrial Enhancements:

  • Processing Speed: Reduced document reading cycles by up to 80% over manual review times.
  • Data Precision: Sustained an automated data ingestion precision rate tracking higher than 95%.
  • Scale Capability: Allowed seamless transaction processing expansion without demanding linear back-office head-count expansion.

Frequently Asked Questions (Industries)

1. How do enterprises and MSMEs partner with the CoE to solve technical challenges?
  • Scoping & Discovery: CoE engineers conduct operational diagnostics with the industry team to extract and map specific manufacturing or process inefficiencies.
  • Challenge Pitching: Vetted problems are opened through formalized tracks like the FUSION 4.0 platform to source matched capabilities from specialized deep-tech innovators.
  • Validation & Execution: The CoE assists throughout prototyping and proof-of-concept alignment cycles to verify performance metrics before factory floor rollouts.
2. What programs does the CoE coordinate to support the manufacturing sector?

Through our flagship FUSION 4.0 Forum (Forum Uniting Startups and Industries On New technologies), we organize sector-focused match-making roundtables, technology demo days, cross-industry knowledge sharing, and structured pilot implementation frameworks tailored for industrial operations.

3. What types of industrial operations are best suited for CoE engagement?

Our frameworks scale effectively across discrete manufacturing, heavy chemicals, energy distribution, engineering fabrication, logistics hubs, and process manufacturing environments looking to build out IoT networks or predictive machine learning capabilities.

4. Who drives the institutional steering and resource governance for the Centre?

The facility operates under a joint governance framework backed by the Department of Science & Technology (DST), Government of Gujarat, Gujarat Informatics Limited (GIL), and NASSCOM, with technology infrastructure support from core ecosystem leaders including Microsoft and Google.
